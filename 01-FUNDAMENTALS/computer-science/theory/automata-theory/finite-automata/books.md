## BEGINNER

### Introduction to the Theory of Computation
Author: Michael Sipser
Year: 2012 (3rd edition)
Publisher: Cengage Learning
Link: https://www.cengage.com/c/introduction-to-the-theory-of-computation-3e-sipser/
Description: Chapters 1-2 provide crystal-clear introduction to finite automata, DFA, NFA, and regular languages.
Why included: Best beginner textbook. Clear proofs and intuitive examples.

### Elements of the Theory of Computation
Author: Harry Lewis, Christos Papadimitriou
Year: 1997
Publisher: Prentice Hall
Link: https://www.amazon.com/Elements-Theory-Computation-Harry-Lewis/dp/0132624788
Description: Accessible introduction to automata theory with excellent illustrations.
Why included: Gentle progression. Good for self-study.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Introduction to Automata Theory, Languages, and Computation
Author: John Hopcroft, Rajeev Motwani, Jeffrey Ullman
Year: 2006 (3rd edition)
Publisher: Pearson
Link: https://www.pearson.com/en-us/subject-catalog/p/introduction-to-automata-theory-languages-and-computation/
Description: Comprehensive coverage of finite automata, NFA/DFA equivalence, and minimization.
Why included: Classic textbook. Industry standard for courses.

### Theory of Computation
Author: Dexter Kozen
Year: 2006
Publisher: Springer
Link: https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/1-84628-477-5
Description: Concise treatment of automata theory with mathematical rigor.
Why included: Elegant proofs. Graduate-level perspective.

### Automata and Computability
Author: Dexter Kozen
Year: 1997
Publisher: Springer
Link: https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-1-4612-1844-9
Description: Earlier Kozen book focusing on automata with clear mathematical approach.
Why included: Accessible yet rigorous. Excellent exercises.

## CLASSIC

### Finite Automata and Their Decision Problems
Author: Michael Rabin, Dana Scott
Year: 1959
Publisher: IBM Journal
Link: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/5392601
Description: Original paper introducing nondeterministic finite automata.
Why included: Foundational work. Historical significance.

### Introduction to Automata Theory, Languages, and Computation (1st ed)
Author: John Hopcroft, Jeffrey Ullman
Year: 1979
Publisher: Addison-Wesley
Link: https://archive.org/details/introductiontoau0000hopc
Description: The original "Cinderella book" - classic automata theory textbook.
Why included: Shaped the field. Still valuable.
