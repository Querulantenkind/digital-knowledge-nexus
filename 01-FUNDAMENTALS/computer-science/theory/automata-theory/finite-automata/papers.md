## INTERMEDIATE

### Finite Automata and Their Decision Problems
Author: Michael Rabin, Dana Scott
Year: 1959
Publisher: IBM Journal of Research and Development
Link: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/5392601
Description: Introduces nondeterministic finite automata and proves equivalence with DFA.
Why included: Foundational paper. Turing Award work.

### A Note on Finite Automata
Author: Stephen Kleene
Year: 1956
Publisher: Automata Studies
Link: https://www.rand.org/pubs/research_memoranda/RM704.html
Description: Kleene's theorem connecting regular expressions and finite automata.
Why included: Fundamental equivalence result.

### On the Synthesis of Finite Automata
Author: Edward Moore
Year: 1956
Publisher: Sequential Machines
Link: https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.5555/1098705
Description: Moore machine model and state minimization.
Why included: Alternative automata model. Practical algorithms.

## ADVANCED

### Minimization of Deterministic Finite Automata
Author: John Hopcroft
Year: 1971
Publisher: Journal of Computer and System Sciences
DOI: 10.1016/S0022-0000(71)80009-3
Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0022000071800093
Description: Hopcroft's O(n log n) algorithm for DFA minimization.
Why included: Optimal algorithm. Still used today.

### State Complexity of Finite Automata
Author: Various authors
Year: 2000-2010
Link: https://arxiv.org/search/?query=state+complexity+finite+automata&searchtype=all
Description: Research on minimal automata size for regular languages.
Why included: Active research area with practical implications.

### Two-Way Finite Automata
Author: John Shepherdson
Year: 1959
Publisher: Journal of the ACM
DOI: 10.1145/320954.320964
Link: https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/320954.320964
Description: Introduction of two-way finite automata.
Why included: Extensions of basic model.
