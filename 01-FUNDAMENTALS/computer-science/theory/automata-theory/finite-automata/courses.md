## BEGINNER

### Introduction to Theoretical Computer Science (Harvard)
Author: Boaz Barak
Year: 2020
Institution: Harvard University
Link: https://introtcs.org/
Description: Free online textbook with chapters on DFA, NFA, and regular languages.
Why included: Modern approach with interactive examples.

### Theory of Computation (MIT 18.404J)
Author: Michael Sipser
Year: 2020
Institution: Massachusetts Institute of Technology
Link: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/18-404j-theory-of-computation-fall-2020/
Description: Complete course starting with finite automata foundations.
Why included: From the master textbook author. Free videos.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Automata Theory (Stanford Online)
Author: Jeffrey Ullman
Year: 2023
Institution: Stanford University
Link: https://online.stanford.edu/courses/soe-ycsautomata-automata-theory
Description: Comprehensive course on finite automata, regular languages, and minimization.
Why included: From legendary automata theorist.

### Computability and Complexity (UC Berkeley CS172)
Author: Luca Trevisan
Year: 2023
Institution: University of California, Berkeley
Link: https://people.eecs.berkeley.edu/~luca/cs172/
Description: Graduate course with strong foundational automata coverage.
Why included: Rigorous treatment with excellent problems sets.

### Formal Languages and Automata (CMU)
Author: Various
Year: 2023
Institution: Carnegie Mellon University
Link: https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~sutner/automata.html
Description: CMU's automata theory course with focus on formal methods.
Why included: Connects automata to verification and logic.
