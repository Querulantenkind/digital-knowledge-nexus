## BEGINNER

### Introduction to Theoretical Computer Science (MIT OpenCourseWare)
Author: Boaz Barak
Year: 2020
Institution: Harvard University / MIT
Link: https://introtcs.org/
Description: Free online textbook and course covering Turing machines from first principles with interactive examples.
Why included: Modern, accessible approach. Free online book with excellent visualizations.

### Automata Theory (Stanford Online)
Author: Jeffrey Ullman
Year: 2023
Institution: Stanford University
Link: https://online.stanford.edu/courses/soe-ycsautomata-automata-theory
Description: Online course covering finite automata leading to Turing machines.
Why included: From legendary automata theorist. Progressive difficulty.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Theory of Computation (MIT 18.404J)
Author: Michael Sipser
Year: 2020
Institution: Massachusetts Institute of Technology
Link: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/18-404j-theory-of-computation-fall-2020/
Description: Complete course from Sipser himself covering automata, computability, and complexity.
Why included: Learn from the textbook author. Comprehensive video lectures and assignments.

### Computability and Complexity (UC Berkeley CS172)
Author: Luca Trevisan
Year: 2023
Institution: University of California, Berkeley
Link: https://people.eecs.berkeley.edu/~luca/cs172/
Description: Graduate-level course on computability theory including advanced Turing machine topics.
Why included: Rigorous treatment from expert instructor.

## ADVANCED

### Advanced Complexity Theory (Princeton COS 522)
Author: Sanjeev Arora, Boaz Barak
Year: 2022
Institution: Princeton University
Link: https://www.cs.princeton.edu/courses/archive/spring22/cos522/
Description: Advanced graduate course exploring computational models beyond standard Turing machines.
Why included: Cutting-edge complexity theory. Oracle machines, interactive proofs.

### Recursion Theory (University of Chicago)
Author: Robert Soare
Year: 2021
Institution: University of Chicago
Link: https://math.uchicago.edu/~soare/
Description: Graduate course on recursion theory and computability from leading researcher.
Why included: Deep dive into Turing degrees and advanced computability.

## CLASSIC

### Introduction to Automata, Languages, and Computation (Coursera)
Author: Jeffrey Ullman
Year: 2014-present
Institution: Stanford University
Link: https://www.coursera.org/learn/automata
Description: Classic course format from Hopcroft-Ullman textbook author.
Why included: Established pedagogy. Used by thousands of students.
