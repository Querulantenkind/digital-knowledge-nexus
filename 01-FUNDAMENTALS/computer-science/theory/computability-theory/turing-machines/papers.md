## BEGINNER

### Turing Machines: An Introduction (Tutorial Paper)
Author: Various educators
Year: 2020
Link: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/turing-machine/
Description: Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy entry providing accessible introduction to Turing machines.

## INTERMEDIATE

### On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem
Author: Alan Turing
Year: 1936
Publisher: Proceedings of the London Mathematical Society
DOI: 10.1112/plms/s2-42.1.230
Link: https://www.cs.virginia.edu/~robins/Turing_Paper_1936.pdf
Description: The original paper defining Turing machines and proving the halting problem undecidable.
Why included: Foundational paper of computer science. Surprisingly readable with Petzold's annotations.

### Computing Machinery and Intelligence
Author: Alan Turing
Year: 1950
Publisher: Mind
DOI: 10.1093/mind/LIX.236.433
Link: https://academic.oup.com/mind/article/LIX/236/433/986238
Description: Introduces the Turing Test and discusses machine intelligence in philosophical context.
Why included: Shows Turing's broader vision. Connects computation to intelligence.

### The Church-Turing Thesis
Author: Alonzo Church
Year: 1936
Publisher: American Journal of Mathematics
Link: https://www.jstor.org/stable/2371045
Description: Church's parallel development of computability through lambda calculus.
Why included: Essential companion to Turing's work. Shows equivalence of models.

## ADVANCED

### Degrees of Unsolvability
Author: Gerald Sacks
Year: 1963
Publisher: Annals of Mathematics Studies
Link: https://press.princeton.edu/books/paperback/9780691079424/degrees-of-unsolvability
Description: Graduate-level treatment of Turing degrees and reducibility hierarchies.
Why included: Advanced computability theory. For researchers in the field.

### Oracle Machines and Relative Computability
Author: Emil Post
Year: 1944
Publisher: Bulletin of the American Mathematical Society
Link: https://projecteuclid.org/journals/bulletin-of-the-american-mathematical-society/volume-50/issue-5/Recursively-enumerable-sets-of-positive-integers-and-their-decision-problems/bams/1183505800.full
Description: Introduces oracle Turing machines and Post's problem.
Why included: Extends Turing machine model. Foundation of complexity theory.

## CLASSIC

### A Machine-Oriented Logic Based on the Resolution Principle
Author: J.A. Robinson
Year: 1965
Publisher: Journal of the ACM
DOI: 10.1145/321250.321253
Link: https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/321250.321253
Description: Connects Turing machines to automated theorem proving.
Why included: Shows practical applications of computability theory.

### Characterizations of the Computable Functions
Author: Stephen Kleene
Year: 1943-1952
Publisher: American Mathematical Society
Link: https://www.ams.org/journals/bull/1981-05-02/S0273-0979-1981-14927-8/
Description: Kleene's alternative characterizations of computable functions.
Why included: Foundational work showing equivalence of different computational models.
