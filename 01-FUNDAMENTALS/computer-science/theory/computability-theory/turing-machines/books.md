## BEGINNER

### Introduction to the Theory of Computation
Author: Michael Sipser
Year: 2013 (3rd edition)
Publisher: Cengage Learning
ISBN: 978-1-133-18779-0
Link: https://www.cengage.com/c/introduction-to-the-theory-of-computation-3e-sipser/
Description: Clear introduction to Turing machines with excellent visualizations and examples before diving into computability theory.
Why included: The gold standard textbook. Chapters 3-4 provide the best introduction to Turing machines for students.

### The Annotated Turing
Author: Charles Petzold
Year: 2008
Publisher: Wiley
ISBN: 978-0470229057
Link: https://www.wiley.com/en-us/The+Annotated+Turing-p-9780470229057
Description: Line-by-line commentary on Alan Turing's 1936 paper with historical context and accessible explanations.
Why included: Makes the original paper accessible. Perfect bridge between history and technical understanding.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Computability and Logic
Author: George Boolos, John Burgess, Richard Jeffrey
Year: 2007 (5th edition)
Publisher: Cambridge University Press
ISBN: 978-0521701464
Link: https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/computability-and-logic/
Description: Comprehensive treatment of Turing machines, recursion theory, and their relationship to mathematical logic.
Why included: Rigorous but readable. Excellent for understanding connections between computation and logic.

### Theory of Computation
Author: Dexter Kozen
Year: 2006
Publisher: Springer
ISBN: 978-1846282973
Link: https://www.springer.com/gp/book/9781846282973
Description: Concise, elegant treatment of automata, computability, and complexity with emphasis on mathematical foundations.
Why included: Mathematically sophisticated but accessible. Great for readers with strong math background.

## ADVANCED

### Computability Theory
Author: S. Barry Cooper
Year: 2004
Publisher: Chapman and Hall/CRC
ISBN: 978-1584882374
Link: https://www.routledge.com/Computability-Theory/Cooper/p/book/9781584882374
Description: Graduate-level comprehensive treatment of computability, degrees of unsolvability, and advanced recursion theory.
Why included: Authoritative modern reference. For serious study of computability theory.

### Classical Recursion Theory (Volume I & II)
Author: Piergiorgio Odifreddi
Year: 1989, 1999
Publisher: North-Holland
ISBN: 978-0444872951
Link: https://www.elsevier.com/books/classical-recursion-theory/odifreddi/978-0-444-87295-1
Description: Encyclopedic two-volume treatment of recursion theory and computability.
Why included: The comprehensive reference. For researchers and advanced graduate students.

## CLASSIC

### On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem
Author: Alan Turing
Year: 1936
Publisher: Proceedings of the London Mathematical Society
Link: https://www.cs.virginia.edu/~robins/Turing_Paper_1936.pdf
Description: The original paper introducing Turing machines and proving the undecidability of the halting problem.
Why included: The founding document of computer science. Essential historical reading.

### Introduction to Automata Theory, Languages, and Computation
Author: John Hopcroft, Rajeev Motwani, Jeffrey Ullman
Year: 2006 (3rd edition)
Publisher: Pearson
ISBN: 978-0321455369
Link: https://www.pearson.com/en-us/subject-catalog/p/Hopcroft-Introduction-to-Automata-Theory-Languages-and-Computation-3rd-Edition/P200000003125
Description: Classic textbook covering automata, Turing machines, and complexity theory comprehensively.
Why included: Industry standard for decades. Used in top CS programs worldwide.

## RECENT (2023+)

### Computability and Complexity: From a Programming Perspective
Author: Neil Jones
Year: 2023 (revised)
Link: https://www.diku.dk/~neil/comp2book.html
Description: Modern perspective connecting classical computability theory to programming language theory.
Why included: Bridges theory and practice. Shows relevance to modern programming.
