## BEGINNER

### Introduction to the Theory of Computation
Author: Michael Sipser
Year: 2012 (3rd edition)
Publisher: Cengage Learning
Link: https://www.cengage.com/c/introduction-to-the-theory-of-computation-3e-sipser/
Description: Chapter 5 covers the halting problem and undecidability with exceptional clarity.
Why included: Best beginner treatment of undecidability. Clear proofs and examples.

### Gödel, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Golden Braid
Author: Douglas Hofstadter
Year: 1979
Publisher: Basic Books
Link: https://www.basicbooks.com/titles/douglas-r-hofstadter/godel-escher-bach/9780465026562/
Description: Explores incompleteness, undecidability, and self-reference through creative dialogues.
Why included: Makes undecidability accessible and fascinating. Pulitzer Prize winner.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Computability and Unsolvability
Author: Martin Davis
Year: 1958
Publisher: Dover Publications
Link: https://store.doverpublications.com/0486614719.html
Description: Classic introduction to undecidability and recursion theory.
Why included: Historical importance. Still highly readable. Affordable Dover edition.

### Introduction to Automata Theory, Languages, and Computation
Author: John Hopcroft, Rajeev Motwani, Jeffrey Ullman
Year: 2006 (3rd edition)
Publisher: Pearson
Link: https://www.pearson.com/en-us/subject-catalog/p/introduction-to-automata-theory-languages-and-computation/
Description: Comprehensive coverage of undecidable problems in formal language theory.
Why included: Standard textbook. Excellent problem sets on Rice's theorem.

### The Nature of Computation
Author: Cristopher Moore, Stephan Mertens
Year: 2011
Publisher: Oxford University Press
Link: https://global.oup.com/academic/product/the-nature-of-computation-9780199233212
Description: Modern treatment connecting undecidability to complexity theory and physics.
Why included: Fresh perspective. Connects many areas. Engaging writing.

---

## ADVANCED

### Computability: Computable Functions, Logic, and the Foundations of Mathematics
Author: Richard Epstein, Walter Carnielli
Year: 2008
Publisher: Wadsworth Publishing
Link: https://www.wadsworth.com/
Description: Graduate-level treatment of computability and undecidability with philosophical depth.
Why included: Rigorous mathematical treatment. Excellent for serious study.

### Classical Recursion Theory
Author: Piergiorgio Odifreddi
Year: 1989-1999 (2 volumes)
Publisher: North-Holland
Link: https://www.elsevier.com/books/classical-recursion-theory/odifreddi/978-0-444-87295-1
Description: Encyclopedic treatment of recursion theory including advanced undecidability results.
Why included: Comprehensive reference. For researchers in computability theory.

### Recursively Enumerable Sets and Degrees
Author: Robert Soare
Year: 1987
Publisher: Springer
Link: https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-3-662-02460-7
Description: Advanced monograph on r.e. sets and Turing degrees.
Why included: Authoritative modern treatment. Essential for specialists.

---

## CLASSIC

### On Formally Undecidable Propositions
Author: Kurt Gödel
Year: 1931 (translated 1962)
Publisher: Dover Publications
Link: https://store.doverpublications.com/0486669807.html
Description: Gödel's original incompleteness theorems paper, connecting logic to undecidability.
Why included: Foundational work. Shows deep connection between logic and computation.

---

## RECENT (2023+)

### The Busy Beaver Frontier (Monograph)
Author: Scott Aaronson and others
Year: 2024
Publisher: arXiv preprints
Link: https://arxiv.org/search/?query=busy+beaver&searchtype=all
Description: Recent work on the busy beaver function and its undecidability.
Why included: Active research area connecting undecidability to concrete problems.
