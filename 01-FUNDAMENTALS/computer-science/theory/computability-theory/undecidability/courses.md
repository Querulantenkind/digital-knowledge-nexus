## BEGINNER

### Introduction to Theoretical Computer Science (Harvard)
Author: Boaz Barak
Year: 2020
Institution: Harvard University
Link: https://introtcs.org/
Description: Free online textbook with chapters on the halting problem and undecidability.
Why included: Modern, accessible treatment. Interactive examples.

### Theory of Computation (MIT 18.404J)
Author: Michael Sipser
Year: 2020
Institution: Massachusetts Institute of Technology
Link: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/18-404j-theory-of-computation-fall-2020/
Description: Complete course covering undecidability and Rice's theorem.
Why included: From the textbook author. Free video lectures.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Computability and Complexity (UC Berkeley CS172)
Author: Luca Trevisan
Year: 2023
Institution: University of California, Berkeley
Link: https://people.eecs.berkeley.edu/~luca/cs172/
Description: Graduate course with strong coverage of undecidability and reducibility.
Why included: Rigorous treatment with excellent problem sets.

### Great Ideas in Theoretical Computer Science (MIT 6.045J)
Author: Various
Year: 2023
Institution: Massachusetts Institute of Technology
Link: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-045j-automata-computability-and-complexity-spring-2005/
Description: Covers undecidability as part of broader theory landscape.
Why included: Contextualizes undecidability among other big ideas.
