## BEGINNER

### A Mind at Play: How Claude Shannon Invented the Information Age
Author: Jimmy Soni, Rob Goodman
Year: 2017
Publisher: Simon & Schuster
Link: https://www.simonandschuster.com/books/A-Mind-at-Play/Jimmy-Soni/9781476766683
Description: Engaging biography of Shannon explaining information theory's origins and impact.
Why included: Makes theory accessible through storytelling. Perfect entry point.

### Information Theory: A Tutorial Introduction
Author: James V. Stone
Year: 2015
Publisher: Sebtel Press
Link: https://www.amazon.com/Information-Theory-Tutorial-Introduction-ebook/dp/B00T5FFWGE
Description: Gentle introduction to information theory with minimal math prerequisites.
Why included: Clear explanations. Builds intuition before formalism.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Elements of Information Theory
Author: Thomas Cover, Joy Thomas
Year: 2006 (2nd edition)
Publisher: Wiley-Interscience
Link: https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Elements+of+Information+Theory

,+2nd+Edition-p-9780471241959
Description: Standard graduate textbook on information theory. Comprehensive and rigorous.
Why included: Industry standard. Used in most graduate courses.

### Information Theory, Inference, and Learning Algorithms
Author: David MacKay
Year: 2003
Publisher: Cambridge University Press
Link: https://www.inference.org.uk/mackay/itila/
Description: Connects information theory to machine learning and inference. Free online.
Why included: Modern perspective. Links theory to practice. Freely available.

### An Introduction to Information Theory: Symbols, Signals and Noise
Author: John Pierce
Year: 1980
Publisher: Dover Publications
Link: https://store.doverpublications.com/0486240614.html
Description: Classic accessible introduction with historical context.
Why included: Engaging writing. Affordable Dover edition.

## ADVANCED

### Information Theory and Network Coding
Author: Raymond Yeung
Year: 2008
Publisher: Springer
Link: https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-0-387-79234-7
Description: Advanced treatment including network coding and modern extensions.
Why included: Cutting-edge topics. Research perspective.

### Entropy and Information Theory
Author: Robert Gray
Year: 2011
Publisher: Springer
Link: https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-1-4419-7970-4
Description: Rigorous mathematical treatment of entropy and information measures.
Why included: Deep mathematical foundations. For specialists.

---

## CLASSIC

### The Mathematical Theory of Communication
Author: Claude Shannon, Warren Weaver
Year: 1949
Publisher: University of Illinois Press
Link: https://pure.mpg.de/rest/items/item_2383164/component/file_2383163/content
Description: Shannon's original 1948 paper plus Weaver's accessible introduction.
Why included: Foundational work. Birth of information theory. Still readable.

### Communication Theory of Secrecy Systems
Author: Claude Shannon
Year: 1949
Publisher: Bell System Technical Journal
Link: https://archive.org/details/bstj28-4-656
Description: Shannon's paper founding modern cryptography using information theory.
Why included: Historical importance. Connects information theory to security.

## RECENT (2023+)

### Information Theory in Modern Applications (Survey)
Author: Various researchers
Year: 2024
Link: https://arxiv.org/search/?query=information+theory+applications&searchtype=all
Description: Recent surveys on information theory in ML, quantum computing, and biology.
Why included: Contemporary applications and extensions.
