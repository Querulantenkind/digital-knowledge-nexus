## INTERMEDIATE

### The Complexity of Theorem-Proving Procedures
Author: Stephen A. Cook
Year: 1971
Publisher: Proceedings of the 3rd Annual ACM Symposium on Theory of Computing
DOI: 10.1145/800157.805047
Link: https://www.cs.toronto.edu/~sacook/homepage/1971.pdf
Description: Seminal paper introducing P, NP, and NP-completeness. Proves SAT is NP-complete.
Why included: Foundational work that created the field. Essential historical and technical reference.

### Reducibility Among Combinatorial Problems
Author: Richard M. Karp
Year: 1972
Publisher: Complexity of Computer Computations
Link: https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Reducibility+Among+Combinatorial+Problems+Karp+1972
Description: Proves 21 different problems are NP-complete. Demonstrates reduction methodology.
Why included: Shows that NP-completeness is not just theoretical—appears everywhere in practice.

## ADVANCED

### Natural Proofs
Author: Alexander A. Razborov, Steven Rudich
Year: 1997
Publisher: Journal of the ACM
DOI: 10.1145/258950.258951
Link: https://www.cs.cmu.edu/afs/cs.cmu.edu/user/odonnell/Public/papers/natural-proofs.pdf
Description: Proves structural barriers to proving P ≠ NP through natural proof methods.
Why included: Explains why certain proof techniques fail. Fundamental theoretical barrier.

### Limits on Proving Formulae in Propositional Calculus
Author: Paul Beame, Russell Impagliazzo, Jan Krajíček, Toniann Pitassi, Pavel Pudlák
Year: 1998
Publisher: Journal of Symbolic Logic
DOI: 10.2307/2586720
Description: Explores proof complexity and connections to P vs NP.
Why included: Alternative angle on why P ≠ NP is hard to prove.

### The Quest for P ≠ NP (Survey)
Author: Avi Wigderson
Year: 2019
Link: https://www.ias.edu/ideas/wigderson-quest-p-versus-np
Description: Modern perspective on the problem and progress from leading complexity theorist.
Why included: Recent research directions and why the problem remains hard.
