## BEGINNER

### Fundamentals of Computer Science (MIT OpenCourseWare)
Author: Erik Demaine, various
Year: 2020
Institution: Massachusetts Institute of Technology
Link: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-0001-introduction-to-computer-science-and-programming-in-python-fall-2016/
Description: Foundational CS course covering computation basics before diving into complexity theory.
Why included: Builds necessary programming and algorithm background for understanding P vs NP.

### Introduction to the Theory of Computation (Online)
Author: Ryan O'Donnell (lecturer)
Year: 2024
Institution: Carnegie Mellon University
Link: https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~odonnell/complexity15/
Description: Complete online course with lecture notes, videos, and homeworks covering P, NP, and NP-completeness.
Why included: Free, comprehensive, from expert instructor. Best intro course available online.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Computational Complexity (MIT OpenCourseWare 6.045J)
Author: Ryan O'Donnell, Avi Wigderson (guest lecturer)
Year: 2016
Institution: Massachusetts Institute of Technology
Link: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-045j-automata-computability-and-complexity-spring-2011/
Description: Advanced course covering P, NP, NP-completeness, and oracle models.
Why included: Video lectures and problem sets from MIT. Rigorous and well-taught.

### The P-NP Problem (Coursera Specialization)
