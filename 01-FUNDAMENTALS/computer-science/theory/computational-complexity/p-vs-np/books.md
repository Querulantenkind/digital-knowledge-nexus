## BEGINNER

### Introduction to the Theory of Computation
Author: Michael Sipser
Year: 2013 (3rd edition)
Publisher: Cengage Learning
ISBN: 978-1-133-18779-0
Link: https://www.cengage.com/c/introduction-to-the-theory-of-computation-3e-sipser/
Description: Comprehensive textbook covering automata, computability, and complexity theory with clear explanations and intuitive examples.
Why included: The most accessible introduction to P vs NP and computational complexity for beginners. Widely used in universities.

### Computational Complexity: A Modern Approach (Chapters 1-3)
Author: Sanjeev Arora, Boaz Barak
Year: 2009
Publisher: Cambridge University Press
ISBN: 978-0521424264
Link: https://www.cambridge.org/us/universitypress/subjects/computer-science/algorithm-and-computational-theory/computational-complexity-modern-approach?format=PB
Description: Beginner-friendly introduction to complexity theory with intuitive motivations before formal definitions.
Why included: Authors excel at building intuition. These chapters provide the foundation without getting bogged down in proofs.

### What is Computational Complexity? (Lectures notes)
Author: Ryan O'Donnell
Year: 2024
Link: https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~odonnell/complexity15/
Description: Free online lecture notes from CMU covering complexity fundamentals.
Why included: Free, authoritative, and teaches by example rather than pure formalism.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Computational Complexity: A Modern Approach (Full)
Author: Sanjeev Arora, Boaz Barak
Year: 2009
Publisher: Cambridge University Press
ISBN: 978-0521424264
Link: https://www.cambridge.org/us/universitypress/subjects/computer-science/algorithm-and-computational-theory/computational-complexity-modern-approach?format=PB
Description: Complete treatment of complexity theory including P, NP, NP-completeness, and advanced topics.
Why included: The definitive modern textbook. Rigorous but readable. Industry standard reference.

### Computers and Intractability: A Guide to the Theory of NP-Completeness
Author: Michael R. Garey, David S. Johnson
Year: 1979
Publisher: W.H. Freeman
ISBN: 978-0716710455
Link: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computers_and_Intractability
Description: Classic reference compendium of NP-complete problems with proofs and practical implications.
Why included: The canonical NP-completeness reference. Still essential despite age. Contains over 300 NP-complete problems.

## CLASSIC

### On the Computational Complexity of Algorithms (1972 original)
Author: Alfred V. Aho, John E. Hopcroft, Jeffrey D. Ullman
Year: 1974 (revised)
Publisher: Addison-Wesley
ISBN: 978-0201000290
Link: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Design_and_Analysis_of_Computer_Algorithms
Description: Foundational textbook that established modern complexity analysis terminology.
Why included: Seminal work that defined Big-O notation and complexity classes. Historically important.

### The P-NP Problem (Clay Mathematics Millennium Prize Problem Description)
Author: Stephen Cook
Year: 2000
Link: https://www.claymath.org/millennium-problems/p-vs-np-problem
Description: Official problem statement and context for one of seven unsolved Millennium Prize Problems.
Why included: Authoritative problem formulation from a founder of the field.
