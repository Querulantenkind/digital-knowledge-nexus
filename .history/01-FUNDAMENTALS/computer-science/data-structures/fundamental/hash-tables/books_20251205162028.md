## BEGINNER

### Introduction to Algorithms (CLRS) - Chapter 11
Author: Thomas H. Cormen, Charles E. Leiserson, Ronald L. Rivest, Clifford Stein
Year: 2009 (3rd Edition)
Publisher: MIT Press
ISBN: 978-0262033848
Link: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262033848/introduction-to-algorithms/
Description: Comprehensive treatment of hash tables including hashing techniques, collision resolution, universal hashing, and analysis of expected operations.
Why included: Authoritative introduction; rigorous proofs of hash table efficiency and collision handling strategies.

### The C Programming Language (Chapter on Data Structures)
Author: Brian W. Kernighan, Dennis M. Ritchie
Year: 1988 (2nd Edition)
Publisher: Prentice Hall
ISBN: 978-0131103627
Link: https://www.pearson.com/
Description: Classic treatment of implementing hash tables with practical C implementations and collision handling.
Why included: Low-level perspective on hash table implementation; influential in systems programming.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Algorithms in C (Chapter on Symbol Tables)
Author: Robert Sedgewick
Year: 1998 (3rd Edition)
Publisher: Addison-Wesley
ISBN: 978-0201314653
Link: https://algs4.cs.princeton.edu/
Description: Detailed explanations of hash table implementation with visual demonstrations and performance analysis.
Why included: Pedagogical excellence; clear code examples and empirical analysis.

## ADVANCED

### The Art of Computer Programming, Volume 3 (Chapters 6)
Author: Donald E. Knuth
Year: 1998 (2nd Edition)
Publisher: Addison-Wesley Professional
ISBN: 978-0201896839
Link: https://www-cs-faculty.stanford.edu/~knuth/taocp.html
Description: Exhaustive mathematical analysis of hashing including collision analysis, load factors, and advanced techniques.
Why included: Ultimate reference; mathematical rigor and comprehensive treatment of all hashing variants.

### Modern Hash Tables
Author: Scott Sanderson (various authors)
Year: 2020s
Publisher: Research papers and surveys
Description: Contemporary analysis of high-performance hash table implementations including cuckoo hashing, robin hood hashing, and performance engineering.
Why included: Practical advances in hash table design for modern systems.

---

## CLASSIC

### Multiplicative Hashing
Author: Donald E. Knuth
Year: 1969
Venue: Seminumerical Algorithms (TAOCP Vol 2)
Description: Foundational analysis of multiplicative hash functions and their properties.
Why included: Historical foundation for modern hash function design.
