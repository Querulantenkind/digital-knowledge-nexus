## BEGINNER

### Randomized Algorithms (Motwani & Raghavan)
Author: Rajeev Motwani, Prabhakar Raghavan
Year: 1995
Publisher: Cambridge University Press
Link: https://www.cambridge.org/
Description: Classic textbook introducing core randomized algorithmic techniques (probabilistic method, tail bounds, basic randomized algorithms).
Why included: Foundational reference for theory and examples; widely used in courses.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Probability and Computing (Mitzenmacher & Upfal)
Author: Michael Mitzenmacher, Eli Upfal
Year: 2005
Publisher: Cambridge University Press
Link: https://www.cambridge.org/
Description: Covers probabilistic methods, concentration bounds, and applications to algorithms and data structures.
Why included: Excellent treatment of probabilistic tools used in randomized algorithms.

## ADVANCED

### Advanced texts and monographs on randomized linear algebra and sublinear algorithms
Author: Varied (recent monographs and survey chapters)
Description: Modern texts covering streaming, sketching, randomized numerical linear algebra, and sublinear-time randomized algorithms.
Why included: Represents research-front techniques where randomness enables scalability.

## CLASSIC

### Foundational papers collection
Description: Seminal works such as Rabin's randomized algorithms, Karger's min-cut, and early Monte Carlo/Las Vegas algorithm papers.
Why included: Historical context and canonical examples demonstrating power of randomness.
