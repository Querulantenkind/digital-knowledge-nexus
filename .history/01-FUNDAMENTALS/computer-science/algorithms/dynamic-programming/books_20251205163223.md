## BEGINNER

### Introduction to Algorithms (CLRS) - Chapters 14-15
Author: Thomas H. Cormen, Charles E. Leiserson, Ronald L. Rivest, Clifford Stein
Year: 2009 (3rd Edition)
Publisher: MIT Press
ISBN: 978-0262033848
Link: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262033848/introduction-to-algorithms/
Description: Classic textbook chapters on dynamic programming introducing rod cutting, matrix-chain multiplication, and longest common subsequence with proofs.
Why included: Foundational reference; rigorous yet accessible introduction to DP principles.

### Algorithm Design Manual (Chapter 8)
Author: Steven S. Skiena
Year: 2020 (3rd Edition)
Publisher: Springer
ISBN: 978-3030542559
Link: https://www.algorist.com/
Description: Practical DP patterns and problem-solving strategies with numerous worked examples and design paradigms.
Why included: Emphasis on pattern recognition and practical problem-solving; excellent for applying DP to new problems.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Dynamic Programming: From Novice to Advanced
Author: Aditya Chari (LeetCode Editorial)
Year: 2023
Publisher: Self-published (available online)
Link: https://leetcode.com/explore/featured/card/dynamic-programming/
Description: Progressive learning path covering foundational DP problems, building to advanced optimizations and techniques.
Why included: Well-curated progression with interactive problem-solving; modern platform approach.

### The Art of Computer Programming, Volume 3 (Section 5.3)
Author: Donald E. Knuth
Year: 1998 (2nd Edition)
Publisher: Addison-Wesley Professional
ISBN: 978-0201896839
Link: https://www-cs-faculty.stanford.edu/~knuth/taocp.html
Description: Exhaustive mathematical analysis of DP, memoization, state space design, and optimization techniques.
Why included: Ultimate reference for deep mathematical understanding of DP theory and applications.

Author: Dimitri P. Bertsekas
Year: 1995
