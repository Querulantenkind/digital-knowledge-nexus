## BEGINNER

### Introduction to Algorithms (CLRS) - Chapter 32 (String Matching)
Author: Thomas H. Cormen, Charles E. Leiserson, Ronald L. Rivest, Clifford Stein
Year: 2009 (3rd Edition)
Publisher: MIT Press
ISBN: 978-0262033848
Link: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262033848/introduction-to-algorithms/
Description: Clear treatment of pattern matching algorithms including naive approach, KMP, and Boyer-Moore with complexity analysis.
Why included: Authoritative introduction; rigorous proofs of algorithm correctness and efficiency.

### Algorithm Design Manual (Chapter 8)
Author: Steven S. Skiena
Year: 2020 (3rd Edition)
Publisher: Springer
ISBN: 978-3030542559
Link: https://www.algorist.com/
Description: Practical guide to pattern matching with emphasis on which algorithms to use for different problem types.
Why included: Problem-oriented approach; clear guidance on algorithm selection.

## INTERMEDIATE

### The Art of Computer Programming, Volume 3 (Section 6.3)
Author: Donald E. Knuth
Year: 1998 (2nd Edition)
Publisher: Addison-Wesley Professional
ISBN: 978-0201896839
Link: https://www-cs-faculty.stanford.edu/~knuth/taocp.html
Description: Exhaustive mathematical treatment of string matching including KMP algorithm derivation and analysis.
Why included: Definitive reference; mathematical rigor with practical algorithms.

## ADVANCED

### Handbook of Exact String Matching Algorithms
Author: Christian Charras, Thierry Lecroq
Year: 2004
Publisher: King's College London
Link: http://www-igm.univ-mlv.fr/~lecroq/string/
Description: Comprehensive reference of string matching algorithms with implementations and comparisons.
Why included: Encyclopedic coverage of algorithm variants and optimizations.

## CLASSIC

### Fast Pattern Matching in Strings
Author: Donald E. Knuth, James H. Morris, Vaughan R. Pratt
Year: 1977
Venue: SIAM Journal on Computing
DOI: 10.1137/0206024
Description: Original KMP (Knuth-Morris-Pratt) algorithm paper establishing linear-time pattern matching.
Why included: Seminal work; revolutionary advancement in string matching complexity.
