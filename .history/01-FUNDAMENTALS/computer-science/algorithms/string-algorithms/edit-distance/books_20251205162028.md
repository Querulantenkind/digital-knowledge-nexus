## BEGINNER

### Introduction to Algorithms (CLRS) - Chapter 15 (Dynamic Programming)
Author: Thomas H. Cormen, Charles E. Leiserson, Ronald L. Rivest, Clifford Stein
Year: 2009 (3rd Edition)
Publisher: MIT Press
ISBN: 978-0262033848
Link: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262033848/introduction-to-algorithms/
Description: Clear treatment of longest common subsequence (related to edit distance) using dynamic programming.
Why included: Foundational reference; rigorous treatment of string DP problems.

### Algorithm Design Manual (Chapter 8)
Author: Steven S. Skiena
Year: 2020 (3rd Edition)
Publisher: Springer
ISBN: 978-3030542559
Link: https://www.algorist.com/
Description: Practical treatment of edit distance with applications in spell-checking and sequence alignment.
Why included: Problem-centric approach; clear guidance on algorithm application.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Biological Sequence Analysis: Probabilistic Models of Proteins and Nucleic Acids
Author: Richard Durbin, Sean R. Eddy, Anders Krogh, Graeme Mitchison
Year: 1998
Publisher: Cambridge University Press
ISBN: 978-0521629713
Link: https://www.cambridge.org/
Description: Application of edit distance and alignment algorithms to biological sequences.
Why included: Real-world applications showing why edit distance matters; connects CS to biology.

## ADVANCED

### The Art of Computer Programming, Volume 3
Author: Donald E. Knuth
Year: 1998 (2nd Edition)
Publisher: Addison-Wesley Professional
ISBN: 978-0201896839
Link: https://www-cs-faculty.stanford.edu/~knuth/taocp.html

## CLASSIC

### The "Longest Common Subsequence" Problem
Author: Wagner, Robert A., Fischer, Michael J.
Year: 1974
Venue: Journal of the ACM
DOI: 10.1145/321796.321811
Description: Foundational paper establishing dynamic programming approach to sequence comparison.
Why included: Seminal work in computational biology and string algorithms.
