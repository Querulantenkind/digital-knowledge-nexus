## BEGINNER

### Introduction to Algorithms (CLRS) — Chapter 12 (Search Trees) and Section 2.3 (Divide and Conquer)
Author: Cormen, Leiserson, Rivest, Stein
Year: 2009 (3rd Edition)
Publisher: MIT Press
Link: [CLRS](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262033848/introduction-to-algorithms/)
Description: Comprehensive coverage of binary search and tree-based search structures with correctness proofs.
Why included: Foundational reference; clear exposition of variants and applications.

### Algorithm Design Manual (Chapter 3)
Author: Steven S. Skiena
Year: 2020 (3rd Edition)
Publisher: Springer
Link: [Algorithm Design Manual](https://www.algorist.com/)
Description: Practical coverage of search techniques including binary search variants and their trade-offs.
Why included: Practical perspective with guidance on when to apply each variant.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Algorithms (Sedgewick & Wayne) — Searching sections
Author: Robert Sedgewick, Kevin Wayne
Year: 2011
Publisher: Addison-Wesley
Link: [Algorithms (4th Edition)](https://algs4.cs.princeton.edu/)
Description: Clear illustrations and implementations of binary search with analysis of variants and optimizations.
Why included: Excellent visualizations and implementations across multiple languages.

## ADVANCED

### Cache-Oblivious Algorithms and Data Structures
Author: Various (survey chapters and research monographs)
Description: Advanced material on tuning binary search for modern cache hierarchies and hardware efficiency.
Why included: Important for understanding performance-aware binary search variants.

## CLASSIC

### Early binary search papers and Knuth's Art of Computer Programming (Vol. 3)
Author: Donald Knuth
Description: Foundational material on searching and sorting with exhaustive coverage of search tree theory.
Why included: Seminal reference for search algorithms and their history.
