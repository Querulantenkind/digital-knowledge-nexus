## BEGINNER

### Introduction to Algorithms (CLRS) — Chapter 8 (Sorting in Linear Time)
Author: Cormen, Leiserson, Rivest, Stein
Year: 2009 (3rd Edition)
Publisher: MIT Press
Link: [CLRS](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262033848/introduction-to-algorithms/)
Description: Comprehensive treatment of counting sort, radix sort, bucket sort with detailed analyses and correctness proofs.
Why included: Authoritative reference; covers theory and practical considerations.

### Algorithm Design Manual (Chapter 4 — Sorting)
Author: Steven S. Skiena
Year: 2020 (3rd Edition)
Publisher: Springer
Link: [Algorithm Design Manual](https://www.algorist.com/)
Description: Practical guidance on non-comparison sorting with applications and trade-offs.
Why included: Problem-oriented perspective; when and why to use non-comparison sorts.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Algorithms (Sedgewick & Wayne) — Sorting sections
Author: Robert Sedgewick, Kevin Wayne
Year: 2011
Publisher: Addison-Wesley
Link: [Algorithms (4th Edition)](https://algs4.cs.princeton.edu/)
Description: Implementations and visualizations of counting, radix, and bucket sorts with complexity analysis.
Why included: Excellent code examples and step-by-step explanations.

Author: Various (survey chapters and research monographs)

## CLASSIC

### Foundational papers on sorting theory and lower bounds
Description: Historical papers establishing lower bounds for comparison-based sorting and introduction of linear-time alternatives.
Why included: Theoretical foundations explaining when and why non-comparison sorts are optimal.
