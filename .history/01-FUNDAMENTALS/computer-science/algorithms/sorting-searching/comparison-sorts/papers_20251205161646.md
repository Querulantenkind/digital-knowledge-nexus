## BEGINNER

### On the Optimality of Optimal Sorting Networks
Author: Ajtai, M., Komlós, J., Szemerédi, E.
Year: 1983
Venue: ACM Symposium on Theory of Computing (STOC)
DOI: 10.1145/800061.808726
Link: https://doi.org/10.1145/800061.808726
Description: Proves that AKS sorting network achieves optimal comparison depth, settling a long-standing question about sorting lower bounds.
Why included: Foundational result on the theoretical limits of comparison-based sorting.

### An Optimal Algorithm for Merge Sorting
Author: Hwang, Frank K., Lin, Shen
Year: 1972
Venue: Journal of the ACM
DOI: 10.1145/321879.321884
Link: https://doi.org/10.1145/321879.321884
Description: Optimal algorithm for merging sorted sequences with minimal comparisons.
Why included: Theoretical analysis of merge sort efficiency; elegant proof technique.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Quicksort is Optimal
Author: Sedgewick, Robert
Year: 1978
Venue: Acta Informatica
DOI: 10.1007/BF00289520
Link: https://doi.org/10.1007/BF00289520
Description: Proves quicksort's optimality in terms of average-case performance and provides detailed complexity analysis.
Why included: Rigorous mathematical treatment of quicksort's behavior; explains why quicksort is practically optimal despite worst-case O(n²).

### A Fast Sorting Algorithm using Parallel Processing
Author: Batcher, Kenneth E.
Year: 1968
Venue: Journal of the ACM
DOI: 10.1145/321495.321503
Link: https://doi.org/10.1145/321495.321503
Description: Introduces bitonic sort, a parallel comparison-based sorting algorithm.
Why included: Pioneering work in parallel sorting; leads to broader understanding of comparison-based paradigm limits.
