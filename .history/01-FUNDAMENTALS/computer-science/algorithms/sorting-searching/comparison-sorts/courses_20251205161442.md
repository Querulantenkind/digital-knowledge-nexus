## BEGINNER

### Algorithms, Part I (Coursera)
Instructor: Robert Sedgewick, Kevin Wayne (Princeton University)
Year: Ongoing
Platform: Coursera
Link: https://www.coursera.org/learn/algorithms-part1
Duration: 6 weeks, ~6 hours/week
Prerequisites: Basic programming knowledge (Java or equivalent)
Description: Comprehensive introduction to algorithms including elementary sorting (selection, insertion, shell), mergesort, and quicksort with live demonstrations.
Why included: Exceptional teaching quality with visual demonstrations; fundamentals course covers comparison sorts thoroughly before advanced techniques.

### Introduction to Computer Science (Harvard CS50)
Instructor: David J. Malan (Harvard University)
Year: Ongoing
Platform: edX / Harvard
Link: https://cs50.harvard.edu/
Duration: 12 weeks, ~10-12 hours/week
Prerequisites: None
Description: Week 3-4 focuses on algorithms and searching/sorting, with particular emphasis on comparison-based sorting algorithms and complexity analysis.
Why included: Accessible yet rigorous introduction; excellent video explanations with visual algorithm demonstrations.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Analysis of Algorithms (Princeton University)
Instructor: Robert Sedgewick, Kevin Wayne
Year: Ongoing
Platform: Coursera / Princeton
Link: https://www.coursera.org/learn/analysis-of-algorithms
Duration: 4 weeks, ~5 hours/week
Prerequisites: Basic algorithms and discrete mathematics
Description: Deep dive into algorithm analysis with focus on sorting algorithms, including complexity proofs and empirical analysis.
Why included: Rigorous mathematical treatment; directly applicable to understanding comparison sort performance.

### Algorithms Specialization (Stanford)
Instructor: Tim Roughgarden
Year: Ongoing
Platform: Coursera
Link: https://www.coursera.org/specializations/algorithms
Duration: 4 courses, ~6 hours/week each
Prerequisites: Discrete mathematics, basic programming
Description: Comprehensive specialization including detailed treatment of merge sort, quicksort, and lower bounds for comparison-based sorting.
Why included: World-class teaching on algorithm fundamentals; particularly strong on asymptotic analysis and practical implementation.

## ADVANCED

### Advanced Algorithms (MIT OpenCourseWare 6.854)
Instructor: David Karger
Year: 2008 (evergreen)
Platform: MIT OpenCourseWare
Link: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-854j-advanced-algorithms-fall-2008/
Duration: Self-paced
Prerequisites: Strong algorithms foundation, discrete math
Description: Graduate-level course covering sorting networks, parallel sorting, and advanced theoretical results on comparison lower bounds.
Why included: State-of-the-art treatment of advanced sorting theory for research-track students.
