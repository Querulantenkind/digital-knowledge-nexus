## BEGINNER

### AVL Trees: A Self-Balancing Binary Search Tree
Author: Adelson-Velsky & Landis
Year: 1962
Description: Seminal paper introducing AVL trees with balanced insertion and deletion algorithms.
Why included: Foundational work on self-balancing structures; clear presentation of balancing rules.

### Binary Search Trees and Balancing Fundamentals
Author: Various survey papers
Description: Accessible papers explaining BST operations, problems, and solutions through balancing.
Why included: Clear motivation for studying self-balancing variants.

---

## INTERMEDIATE

### Red-Black Trees: Rebalancing Algorithms and Implementation
Author: Bayer (original work)
Year: 1972-1978
Description: Papers introducing and analyzing red-black tree variants and their properties.
Why included: Understanding efficient rebalancing with O(1) amortized rotations.

### B-Trees and Multi-Way Search Trees
Author: Bayer, McCreight
Year: 1972
Description: Foundational paper on B-trees for database systems and external memory.
Why included: Practical data structure for I/O-efficient searching.

### Treaps and Randomized Search Trees
Author: Aragon & Seidel
Year: 1989
Description: Novel approach combining random priorities with BST properties.
Why included: Elegant probabilistic balancing technique.

---

## ADVANCED

### Splay Trees: Self-Adjusting Binary Search Trees
Author: Sleator & Tarjan
Year: 1985
Description: Research on adaptive tree structures with amortized O(log n) operations.
Why included: Advanced balancing technique for access patterns.

### Persistent and Functional Search Trees
Author: Driscoll, Sarnak, Sleator, Tarjan
Year: 1986
Description: Techniques for maintaining version history in tree structures.
Why included: Modern applications in functional programming and databases.

### Foundational papers on tree rotations and invariants
Description: Early work establishing theoretical foundations of tree balancing.
