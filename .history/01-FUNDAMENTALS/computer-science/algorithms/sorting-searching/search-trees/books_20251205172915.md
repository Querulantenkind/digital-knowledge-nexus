## BEGINNER

### Introduction to Algorithms (CLRS) — Chapters 12-13 (Search Trees)
Author: Cormen, Leiserson, Rivest, Stein
Year: 2009 (3rd Edition)
Publisher: MIT Press
Link: [CLRS](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262033848/introduction-to-algorithms/)
Description: Comprehensive treatment of binary search trees, red-black trees, B-trees with correctness proofs and complexity analysis.
Why included: Authoritative reference; covers theory and practical considerations.

### Algorithm Design Manual (Chapter 3.4 — Binary Search Trees)
Author: Steven S. Skiena
Year: 2020 (3rd Edition)
Publisher: Springer
Link: [Algorithm Design Manual](https://www.algorist.com/)
Description: Practical guidance on search tree design, balancing strategies, and applications.
Why included: Problem-oriented perspective; when and why to use different tree types.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Algorithms (Sedgewick & Wayne) — Chapters 3.2-3.3 (Balanced Search Trees)
Author: Robert Sedgewick, Kevin Wayne
Year: 2011
Publisher: Addison-Wesley
Link: [Algorithms (4th Edition)](https://algs4.cs.princeton.edu/)
Description: Implementations of BST, AVL trees, red-black trees with visualization and complexity analysis.
Why included: Excellent code examples and step-by-step explanations.

### The Art of Computer Programming Vol. 3 (Searching and Sorting)
Author: Donald E. Knuth
Year: 1998
Publisher: Addison-Wesley
Link: [TAOCP Vol. 3](https://www-cs-faculty.stanford.edu/~knuth/taocp.html)
Description: Exhaustive mathematical treatment of search trees and their properties.
Why included: Deep theoretical understanding; classic reference.

## ADVANCED

### Persistent Data Structures and Search Trees
Author: Various research monographs
Description: Advanced techniques for functional/immutable search tree implementations and versioning.
Why included: Modern applications in functional programming and database systems.

### Tree Rotations and Rebalancing Algorithms
Description: Research on efficient rebalancing strategies and amortized analysis.
Why included: Understanding performance characteristics of self-balancing trees.

## CLASSIC

### Foundational papers on AVL and red-black trees
Description: Original papers introducing AVL trees (Adelson-Velsky, Landis) and red-black trees (Bayer).
Why included: Historical foundations; understanding design motivation.
