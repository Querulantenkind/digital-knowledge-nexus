## BEGINNER

### Introduction to Algorithms (CLRS) - Sections on Greedy Algorithms
Author: Thomas H. Cormen, Charles E. Leiserson, Ronald L. Rivest, Clifford Stein
Year: 2009 (3rd Edition)
Publisher: MIT Press
Link: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262033848/introduction-to-algorithms/
Description: Standard graduate/advanced-undergraduate text covering greedy algorithms (activity selection, Huffman coding) with correctness proofs and exercises.
Why included: Authoritative treatment; excellent mix of theory and worked examples for beginners progressing to rigorous proofs.

### The Algorithm Design Manual (Chapter on Greedy Algorithms)
Author: Steven S. Skiena
Year: 2020 (3rd Edition)
Publisher: Springer
Link: https://www.algorist.com/
Description: Friendly, example-driven exposition of common greedy techniques and when they apply.
Why included: Practical perspective and many example problems for learning applied greedy strategies.

## ADVANCED

### Matroid Theory and Combinatorial Optimization (selected chapters)
Author: Various (survey chapters)
Description: Advanced resources that connect greedy algorithms to matroid theory and characterize when greedy approaches are optimal.
Why included: Provides the theoretical framework (matroids) explaining the correctness of greedy approaches on broad problem classes.

### Algorithmic Game Theory / Optimization texts (Greedy in approximation algorithms)
Author: Varied
Description: Advanced chapters discussing greedy heuristics, approximation bounds, and proofs of (in)approximability.
Why included: Important for understanding limits of greedy strategies and designing approximation guarantees.

---

## CLASSIC

### Matroid theory foundations
Author: Jack Edmonds (and subsequent work)
Description: Seminal work linking matroids and greedy algorithms, establishing formal conditions when the greedy method yields optimal solutions.
Why included: Fundamental theoretical foundation for generalizing greedy correctness beyond ad-hoc proofs.
