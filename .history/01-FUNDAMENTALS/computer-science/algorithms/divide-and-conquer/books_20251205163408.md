## BEGINNER

### Introduction to Algorithms (CLRS) - Divide and Conquer Chapters
Author: Cormen, Leiserson, Rivest, Stein
Year: 2022 (4th Edition)
ISBN: 978-0262046305
Link: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262046305/introduction-to-algorithms/
Description: Comprehensive treatment of divide-and-conquer paradigm including merge sort, quicksort, and master theorem. Chapters 2, 4, 7 provide foundational understanding with mathematical analysis.
Why included: Gold standard textbook; rigorous yet accessible explanations.

### Algorithms (Sedgewick & Wayne)
Author: Robert Sedgewick, Kevin Wayne
Year: 2011 (4th Edition)
ISBN: 978-0321573513
Link: https://algs4.cs.princeton.edu/
Description: Practical approach to divide-and-conquer algorithms with Java implementations. Covers merge sort, quicksort, and binary search with clear visualizations.
Why included: Companion to Princeton Coursera course; implementation-focused.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Algorithm Design Manual
Author: Steven S. Skiena
Year: 2020 (3rd Edition)
ISBN: 978-3030542559
Link: https://www.algorist.com/
Description: War stories and practical applications of divide-and-conquer. Strong focus on problem-solving strategies and real-world usage patterns.
Why included: Bridges theory and practice; excellent problem catalog.

### Algorithms Illuminated (Part 1)
Author: Tim Roughgarden
Year: 2017
ISBN: 978-0999282908
Link: http://www.algorithmsilluminated.org/
Description: Clear explanations of merge sort, counting inversions, Strassen matrix multiplication, and master method with numerous examples.
Why included: Based on Stanford course; pedagogically excellent.

## ADVANCED

### The Art of Computer Programming, Volume 3: Sorting and Searching
Author: Donald E. Knuth
Year: 1998 (2nd Edition)
ISBN: 978-0201896855
Link: https://www-cs-faculty.stanford.edu/~knuth/taocp.html
Description: Comprehensive mathematical analysis of divide-and-conquer sorting algorithms including variants and optimizations.
Why included: Definitive reference; mathematical depth unmatched.

### Introduction to Parallel Algorithms
Author: Joseph JáJá
Year: 1992
ISBN: 978-0201548563
Link: https://www.elsevier.com/books/introduction-to-parallel-algorithms/jaja/978-0-201-54856-3
Description: Parallel divide-and-conquer algorithms for multiprocessor systems with complexity analysis.
Why included: Parallel computing perspective; scalability considerations.

## CLASSIC

### The Design and Analysis of Computer Algorithms
Author: Aho, Hopcroft, Ullman
Year: 1974
ISBN: 978-0201000290
Link: https://www.pearson.com/
Description: Seminal text introducing divide-and-conquer methodology with FFT, Strassen's algorithm, and master theorem.
Why included: Historical foundation; established the paradigm.
