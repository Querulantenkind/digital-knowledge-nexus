## BEGINNER

### Algorithms, Part I (Princeton/Coursera)
Instructor: Robert Sedgewick, Kevin Wayne
Platform: Coursera
Year: 2012-Present
Link: https://www.coursera.org/learn/algorithms-part1
Description: Comprehensive introduction to merge sort, quicksort, and divide-and-conquer thinking with Java implementations.
Why included: Highly rated MOOC; excellent visualizations and assignments.

### Introduction to Algorithms (MIT 6.006)
Instructor: Erik Demaine, Srini Devadas
Platform: MIT OpenCourseWare
Year: 2011-Present
Link: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-006-introduction-to-algorithms-fall-2011/
Description: MIT's foundational algorithms course covering divide-and-conquer with problem sets.
Why included: Gold standard education; rigorous theoretical treatment.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Design and Analysis of Algorithms (Stanford)
Instructor: Tim Roughgarden
Platform: Coursera
Year: 2013-Present
Link: https://www.coursera.org/specializations/algorithms
Description: Deep dive into divide-and-conquer including master method, Karatsuba multiplication, and closest pair problems.
Why included: Excellent instructor; theoretical depth with practical insights.

### Algorithms Specialization (UC San Diego/Coursera)
Instructor: Alexander S. Kulikov, Michael Levin
Platform: Coursera
Year: 2016-Present
Link: https://www.coursera.org/specializations/data-structures-algorithms
Description: Covers divide-and-conquer patterns with emphasis on competitive programming applications.
Why included: Problem-solving focus; extensive practice problems.
