## BEGINNER

### Ford & Fulkerson - Max Flow Foundations
Author: L.R. Ford Jr., D.R. Fulkerson
Year: 1956
Venue: Classic technical report and related papers
Link: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ford%E2%80%93Fulkerson_algorithm
Description: Introduced the augmenting path method that underpins many max-flow algorithms.
Why included: Historical and conceptual foundation for flow algorithms.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Edmonds–Karp (1972)
Author: Edmonds & Karp
Year: 1972
Venue: Journal papers
Description: Analysis of BFS-augmented implementation guaranteeing polynomial time.
Why included: Standard undergraduate-level algorithm with a clear runtime bound.

### Push–Relabel (Goldberg & Tarjan)
Author: A.V. Goldberg, R.E. Tarjan
Year: 1988
Venue: Journal publications
Description: Efficient practical algorithm for large graphs; often best in practice.
Why included: High-performance algorithm essential for modern implementations.
