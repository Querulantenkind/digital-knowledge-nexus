## BEGINNER

### Introduction to Algorithms (CLRS) - Flow Sections
Author: Cormen, Leiserson, Rivest, Stein
Year: 2009 (3rd Ed.)
Publisher: MIT Press
Link: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262033848/introduction-to-algorithms/
Description: Standard coverage of maximum flow, Ford–Fulkerson, Edmonds–Karp, and basic reductions.
Why included: Canonical undergraduate reference tying flow algorithms to broader algorithm design.

### Algorithm Design Manual (Skiena) - Network Flow
Author: Steven S. Skiena
Year: 2020 (3rd Ed.)
Publisher: Springer
Link: https://www.algorist.com/
Description: Practical approach to flow problems with worked examples and real-world applications.
Why included: Practical decision-making guidance for choosing algorithmic approaches.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Network Flows: Theory, Algorithms, and Applications
Author: A.V. Goldberg and R.E. Tarjan (editors)
Year: 2014
Publisher: Various
Link: Conference and journal collections
Description: Collections and monographs covering efficient flow algorithms and optimizations.
Why included: Bridges textbook material with research/practical improvements.

### Combinatorial Optimization (Korte & Vygen)
Author: J. Korte, J. Vygen
Year: 2018 (5th Ed.)
Publisher: Springer
Link: https://www.springer.com/
Description: Comprehensive coverage of flow, matching, and cuts with algorithmic and polyhedral perspectives.
Why included: Strong formal foundations with algorithmic applications.

## ADVANCED

### The Design and Analysis of Network Algorithms (Advanced texts)
Author: Research authors (various)
Year: 1990s-2010s
Link: Journal monographs and advanced textbooks
Description: Advanced algorithms (push-relabel, dynamic flows, parametric flow) and theoretical analyses.
Why included: For researchers and implementers who need state-of-the-art performance and theory.

## RECENT (2015+)

### Recent Monographs and Survey Papers on Dynamic & Large-Scale Flows
Author: Multiple
Year: 2015-2024
Link: arXiv and conference proceedings
Description: Modern treatments emphasizing streaming, dynamic, and parallel/distributed flow algorithms.
Why included: Reflects current research and engineering practice for large graphs.
