## BEGINNER

### Introduction to Graph Theory (Chartrand & Zhang)
Author: Gary Chartrand, Ping Zhang
Year: 2012 (2nd Edition)
Publisher: McGraw-Hill
Link: https://www.pearson.com/
Description: Accessible introduction to graph theory fundamentals including coloring basics.
Why included: Gentle introduction for readers new to graph theory and coloring.

### Graph Theory with Applications
Author: J.A. Bondy and U.S.R. Murty
Year: 1976
Publisher: North-Holland
Link: http://www.graph-theory.com/
Description: Classic textbook with practical examples and exercises on graph coloring.
Why included: Foundational examples and historical perspective.

---

## INTERMEDIATE

### Graph Colouring and the Probabilistic Method
Author: Michael Molloy, Bruce Reed
Year: 2002
Publisher: Springer
Link: https://link.springer.com/
Description: Focused text on coloring techniques, probabilistic methods, and constructive algorithms.
Why included: Bridges combinatorial techniques with algorithmic approaches.

### Algorithmic Graph Theory and Perfect Graphs
Author: Martin Charles Golumbic
Year: 2004
Publisher: Elsevier
Link: https://www.elsevier.com/
Description: Covers algorithmic aspects and special graph classes where coloring is tractable.
Why included: Practical algorithms and theory for structured graph classes.

## ADVANCED

### Graph Coloring: Algorithms and Applications
Author: Tomas Feder, others (collection)
Year: 2010s
Publisher: Various
Link: https://www.springer.com/
Description: Research monographs and collected works on advanced coloring algorithms and complexity.
Why included: For readers pursuing research or advanced algorithm design.

### The Probabilistic Method (Alon & Spencer)
Author: Noga Alon, Joel H. Spencer
Year: 2008
Publisher: Wiley
ISBN: 978-0470170208
Description: Core probabilistic techniques often used in coloring proofs and randomized algorithms.
Why included: Essential techniques for modern graph coloring research.

## RECENT (2015+)

### Recent Surveys and Monographs on Graph Coloring
Author: Various
Year: 2015-2024
Link: Survey papers on arXiv and journal special issues
Description: Contemporary treatments covering distributed coloring, approximate coloring, and parameterized methods.
Why included: Highlights current research directions and practical algorithms.
