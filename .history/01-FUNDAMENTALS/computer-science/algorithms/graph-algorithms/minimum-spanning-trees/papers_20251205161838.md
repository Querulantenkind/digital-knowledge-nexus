## BEGINNER

### On the Shortest Spanning Subtree of a Graph and the Traveling Salesman Problem
Author: Joseph B. Kruskal
Year: 1956
Venue: Proceedings of the American Mathematical Society
DOI: 10.1090/S0002-9939-1956-0078686-7
Link: https://www.ams.org/journals/proc/
Description: Original Kruskal's algorithm paper establishing the correctness of greedy approach to MST.
Why included: Seminal work; elegant proof of greedy correctness.

### Prim's Algorithm
Author: Robert C. Prim
Year: 1957
Venue: Journal of the Association for Computing Machinery (JACM)
DOI: 10.1145/321328.321335
Link: https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/321328.321335
Description: Early MST algorithm adapting Dijkstra's shortest path approach to spanning trees.
Why included: Foundational alternative to Kruskal; shows algorithmic innovation.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Efficient Minimum Spanning Tree Algorithms
Author: Tarjan, Robert Endre
Year: 1983
Venue: Journal of the ACM
DOI: 10.1145/2402.322418
Link: https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/2402.322418
Description: Analysis of MST algorithms including union-find data structures and improved complexity bounds.
Why included: Comprehensive algorithmic analysis; introduces important supporting data structures.

### A Linear-Time Algorithm for Finding Minimum Spanning Forests
Author: Cheriton, David, Tarjan, Robert E.
Year: 1976
Venue: Journal of the ACM
DOI: 10.1145/321921.321932
Link: https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/321921.321932
Description: Theoretical analysis of MST with optimal data structures achieving linear time.
Why included: Optimal algorithm theory; shows importance of data structure choice.
