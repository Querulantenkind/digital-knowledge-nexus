## BEGINNER

### Algorithms, Part II (Coursera - Graph Algorithms Week)
Instructor: Robert Sedgewick, Kevin Wayne (Princeton)
Year: Ongoing
Platform: Coursera
Link: https://www.coursera.org/learn/algorithms-part2
Duration: Week 2-3 of course, ~6-7 hours
Prerequisites: Algorithms Part I (or equivalent)
Description: Dedicated module on minimum spanning trees with visual demonstrations, Kruskal's and Prim's algorithms.
Why included: Princeton's excellent algorithmic teaching; clear visual execution of MST algorithms.

### Graph Traversal and Trees (YouTube Educational)
Instructor: Various educators
Year: 2023
Platform: YouTube
Link: https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=mst+algorithms
Duration: 8-12 hours total
Prerequisites: Basic graphs knowledge
Description: Collection of lectures explaining MST concepts and algorithms from multiple pedagogical perspectives.
Why included: Accessible free resources; multiple explanations help deeper understanding.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Advanced Algorithms (Stanford Specialization - Week 4)
Instructor: Tim Roughgarden
Year: Ongoing
Platform: Coursera
Link: https://www.coursera.org/specializations/algorithms
Duration: 1-2 weeks, ~6-7 hours
Prerequisites: Algorithms fundamentals, discrete mathematics
Description: In-depth analysis of MST algorithms including theoretical complexity and practical optimizations.
Why included: Rigorous theoretical treatment from algorithm design expert.

### Data Structures and Graph Algorithms (CMU 15-451)
Instructor: Ryan O'Donnell, others
Year: Ongoing
Platform: CMU / OpenCourseWare
Link: https://www.cs.cmu.edu/afs/cs/academic/class/15451-f16/
Duration: Full semester course
Prerequisites: Algorithms course, strong math background
Description: Graduate-level treatment of graph algorithms including advanced MST techniques.
Why included: Top-tier computer science program; rigorous mathematical foundation.
