## BEGINNER

### Introduction to Algorithms (CLRS) - Chapter 23
Author: Thomas H. Cormen, Charles E. Leiserson, Ronald L. Rivest, Clifford Stein
Year: 2009 (3rd Edition)
Publisher: MIT Press
ISBN: 978-0262033848
Link: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262033848/introduction-to-algorithms/
Description: Comprehensive treatment of minimum spanning trees including Kruskal's and Prim's algorithms with correctness proofs and complexity analysis.
Why included: Authoritative introduction; well-structured progression from greedy algorithms to MST theory.

### Algorithm Design Manual (Chapter 6)
Author: Steven S. Skiena
Year: 2020 (3rd Edition)
Publisher: Springer
ISBN: 978-3030542559
Link: https://www.algorist.com/
Description: Clear explanations of MST algorithms with practical guidance on algorithm selection and implementation trade-offs.
Why included: Practical perspective; excellent for understanding when to apply which algorithm.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Algorithms in C: Parts 1-4 (Chapter on Graphs)
Author: Robert Sedgewick
Year: 1998 (3rd Edition)
Publisher: Addison-Wesley
ISBN: 978-0201314653
Link: https://algs4.cs.princeton.edu/
Description: Detailed treatment of MST with implementations, visual demonstrations, and empirical performance analysis.
Why included: Pedagogical excellence; implementations and visualizations make abstract concepts concrete.

## ADVANCED

### Optimal Spanning Trees and Shortest Paths
Author: Ellis Horowitz, Sartaj Sahni
Year: 1978
Publisher: Computer Science Press
Description: Advanced theoretical treatment of MST algorithms including lower bounds and optimal data structures.
Why included: Complexity-theoretic perspective on algorithmic optimality.

### Fast Algorithms for Finding Nearest Common Ancestors
Author: Baruch Awerbuch
Year: 1985
Venue: ACM Transactions on Algorithms
Description: Research on efficient MST algorithms and related problems in distributed settings.
Why included: Research frontier in MST optimization.

---

## CLASSIC

### On the Shortest Spanning Subtree of a Graph and the Traveling Salesman Problem
Author: Joseph B. Kruskal
Year: 1956
Venue: Proceedings of the American Mathematical Society
DOI: 10.1090/S0002-9939-1956-0078686-7
Description: Original Kruskal's algorithm paper establishing MST theory and proving correctness of greedy approach.
Why included: Seminal work; foundational for greedy algorithm theory.

### Prim's Algorithm in the First Computers
Author: Robert C. Prim
Year: 1957
Venue: Journal of the Association for Computing Machinery
Description: Early MST algorithm adapting Dijkstra's approach to the spanning tree problem.
Why included: Historical importance; shows development of MST theory.
