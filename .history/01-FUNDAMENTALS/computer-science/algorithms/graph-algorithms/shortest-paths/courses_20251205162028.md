## BEGINNER

### Algorithms, Part I (Coursera)
Instructor: Robert Sedgewick, Kevin Wayne (Princeton)
Year: Ongoing
Platform: Coursera
Link: https://www.coursera.org/learn/algorithms-part1
Duration: 6 weeks, ~6 hours/week
Prerequisites: Basic programming
Description: Comprehensive course including weeks on shortest paths with live code demonstrations and visual algorithm execution.
Why included: World-class teaching; visualizations make abstract algorithm behavior concrete.

### Graph Algorithms (CS50 Decision D)
Instructor: David J. Malan
Year: Ongoing
Platform: edX / Harvard
Link: https://cs50.harvard.edu/
Duration: Self-paced section, ~3-4 hours
Prerequisites: None
Description: Introduction to graph algorithms including BFS/DFS leading to shortest paths with clear visual explanations.
Why included: Accessible introduction; excellent pedagogical progression.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Advanced Algorithms (Stanford)
Instructor: Tim Roughgarden
Year: Ongoing
Platform: Coursera
Link: https://www.coursera.org/specializations/algorithms
Duration: 4-course specialization, ~6 hours/week
Prerequisites: Discrete math, algorithms fundamentals
Description: Comprehensive coverage of shortest path algorithms with proofs, complexity analysis, and applications.
Why included: Top-tier computer science education; rigorous mathematical treatment.

### Graph Algorithms Analysis (MIT 6.851)
Instructor: Erik Demaine
Year: 2012
Platform: MIT OpenCourseWare
Link: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-851-advanced-data-structures-spring-2012/
Duration: 15 weeks
Prerequisites: Strong algorithms background
Description: Graduate-level course covering advanced graph algorithms including shortest paths with theoretical depth.
Why included: Research-level treatment from complexity theory pioneer.
