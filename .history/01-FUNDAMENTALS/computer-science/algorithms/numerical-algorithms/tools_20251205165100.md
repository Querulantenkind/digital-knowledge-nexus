## TOOLS

### NumPy / SciPy (Python)
Link: https://numpy.org/ and https://scipy.org/
Description: Core Python libraries providing a wide range of numerical routines and linear algebra wrappers.
Why included: Standard tooling for prototyping and applied numerical work in Python.

### MATLAB and Octave
Link: https://www.mathworks.com/ and https://www.gnu.org/software/octave/
Description: Commercial and open-source platforms for numerical computing with extensive built-in functions.
Why included: Widely used in engineering education and rapid numerical prototyping.

### Julia and its numerical ecosystem (LinearAlgebra, IterativeSolvers, etc.)
Link: https://julialang.org/
Description: High-performance language designed for numerical computing with growing package ecosystem.
Why included: Provides high-performance alternatives and modern tooling for numerical algorithms.

### PETSc / Trilinos (HPC solvers)
Link: https://petsc.org/ and https://trilinos.github.io/
Description: Large-scale, production-ready libraries for solving PDEs and large linear systems on HPC systems.
Why included: Essential for scalable scientific computing and research-grade numerical work.
