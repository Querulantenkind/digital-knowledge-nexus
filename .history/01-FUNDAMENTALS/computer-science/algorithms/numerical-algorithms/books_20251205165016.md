## BEGINNER

### Numerical Recipes: The Art of Scientific Computing
Author: William H. Press, Saul A. Teukolsky, William T. Vetterling, Brian P. Flannery
Year: 2007 (3rd Edition)
Publisher: Cambridge University Press
Link: https://numerical.recipes/
Description: Practical, example-driven introduction to a broad range of numerical methods with code samples in multiple languages.
Why included: Very approachable for practitioners; contains working code and clear explanations for many core algorithms.

## INTERMEDIATE

### Matrix Computations
Author: Gene H. Golub, Charles F. Van Loan
Year: 2013 (4th Edition)
Publisher: Johns Hopkins University Press
Link: https://jhupbooks.press.jhu.edu/
Description: Comprehensive treatment of numerical linear algebra including algorithms for matrix factorizations and eigenproblems.
Why included: Essential for learners focused on computational linear algebra and performance-aware implementations.

### Applied Numerical Linear Algebra
Author: James W. Demmel
Year: 1997
Publisher: SIAM
Description: Focus on numerical stability and performance in linear algebra computations; bridges theory and high-performance practice.
Why included: Useful for engineers implementing robust numerical algorithms at scale.
